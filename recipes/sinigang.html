Sinigang na Bangus

Sinigang is a beloved traditional dish in the Philippines, widely recognized for its unique sour and savory flavor. Believed to have pre-colonial roots, Sinigang reflects the Filipino palate's natural inclination for sourness, using native ingredients like tamarind, guava, or calamansi as souring agents. Though its exact origin is hard to trace, Sinigang is deeply embedded in Filipino food culture and is considered a staple in many households. Over the years, it has gained popularity not only across different regions of the Philippines with each having its own variation but also among international food lovers, often celebrated as one of the best soups in the world.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole Bangus (milkfish)
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 1 pack tamarind mix
  • 1 radish (labanos)
  • 1 eggplant
  • 1 bundle string bins
  • 1-2 pieces green chili (siling haba)
  • 1 bundle kangkong (water spinach)
  • 6 cups of water
  • Salt or fish sauce (patis)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Ingredients.
    Clean the bangus. Chop the vegetables as needed.

  2. Boil water in a pot
    Add the onion and tomatoes. Let them boil for about 3-5 minutes to release their flavor

  3. Add the radish and eggplant
    Cook for another 3-5 minutes or until slightly tender

  4. Add the bangus slices
    Simmer gently for 8-t0 minutes or until the fish is cooked through. Avoid stirring too much to keep the fish intact

  5. Add the tamarind mix or fresh tamarind juice
    Adjust the sourness to your preference

  6. Add the string beans and green chili
    Cook for another 2-3 minutes

  7. Season with salt or fish sauce
    Taste and adjust according to your preference

  8. Add the kangkong last
    Let it cook for 1-2 minutes or until just wilted

  9. Turn off the heat and serve hot
    Best enjoyed with steamed recipes
