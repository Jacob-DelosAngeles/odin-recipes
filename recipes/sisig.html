Sizzling Sisig

Sizzling Sisig is a beloved Filipino dish known for its bold flavors and sizzling presentation. Traditionally made from finely chopped pork parts often including the cheeks, ears, and liver—it's seasoned with calamansi, onions, and chili, then served on a hot cast-iron plate that keeps it crackling at the table. With its savory, tangy, and slightly spicy taste, Sisig is a popular pulutan (beer match) and a must-try comfort food that showcases the creativity and resourcefulness of Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice
  • 1-2 pieces red chili
  • 1/4 cup liver spread or chopped chicken liver
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • 1 egg
  • Butter or margarine

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients
    Boil the pork belly until tender, the let it cool. Chop the pork into small pieces. Finely chop th onions, garlic, and chili

  2. Grill or fry the pork
    Grill or pan-fry the chopped pork until crispy and golden brown for extra texture and flavor.

  3. Sauté garlic and onions
    In a pan, heat a bit of oil and sauté the garlic until fragrant. Add the chopped onions and cook until slightly translucent.

  4. Add the chopped pork
    Toss in the crispy pork pieces and mix well with the sautéed garlic and onions

  5. Season the sisig
    Add soy sauce, calamansi juice, and chopped chillies. Stir to combine. You may also add liver spread
    or chopped liver at this stage for added richness

  6. Add mayonaise (optional)
    Mix in a spoonful of mayonnaise if you prefer a creamier texture. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.

  7. Transfer to a sizzling plate
    Heat a cast-iron sizzling plate and melt a bit of butter or margarine on it. Transfer the sisig mixture
    onto the hot plate

  8. Crack an egg on top
    Crack an egg over the hot sisig. Let it cook slightly, then mix it in just before serving if desired.

  9. Serve sizzling hot
    Enjoy the steamed rice or as pulutan with a cold drink!
