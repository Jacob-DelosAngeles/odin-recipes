Pork Adobo

Pork Adobo is one of the most iconic and widely recognized dishes in
Filipino cuisine. Known for its savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor,
Pork Adobo is traditionally made by simmering pork in a mixture of soy
sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Its roots trace
back to pre-colonial Philippines, where indigenous people preserved meat
using vinegar and salt—a method later influenced by Spanish culinary
techniques during colonization. Over time, adobo evolved into a national
favorite with countless regional and family variations. Loved for its rich
flavor and long shelf life, Pork Adobo is a symbol of home-style Filipino
cooking and is often served with steamed rice for a truly satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork shoulder
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 head garlic
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercons
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt
  • Boiled eggs (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients
    Cut pork. Crush garlic, slice onions, and measure out other Ingredients

  2. Sauté garlic and onions
    Heat a bit of oil in a pan. Add the garlic and cook until lightly browned. Add onions until soft

  3. Add the pork
    Toss in the pork pieces. Cook until lightly browned on all sides to help seal in flavor

  4. Pour in the Soy Sauce and Vinegar
    Add soy sauce first, followed by vinegar. Do not stir. Let the vinegar cook off its raw taste for about 2-3 minutes

  5. Add bay leaves and peppercorns
    Toss in a few dried bay leaves and whole black peppercorns for aroma and spice.

  6. Pour in water and simmer
    Add enough water to cover the meat. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
    Cover and cook for 40-50 minutes or until pork is tender.

  7. Uncover and reduce the sauce
    Remove the lid and let the sauce reduce until slightly thickened and rich in flavor.

  8. Taste and adjust seasoning
    Add more soy sauce or pinch of sugar if needed. You can also hard-boiled eggs or potatoes at this stage.

  9. Serve hot
    Enjoy the steamed rice and a side of sliced tomatoes or pickled vegetables.
